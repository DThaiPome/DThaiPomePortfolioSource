Summary:

This project is a group-driven bullet hell game, in which the flow of time depends on how fast the player is moving. When the player stops moving, time stops progressing, and vice versa. The project currently has no title, and is still in development.

I am currently responsible for most of the programming, including player controls, enemy behaviours, and other systems. The most prominent system present in the game is its way of handling the many bullet and enemy objects that are constantly spawned. In the game, enemies and the player can potentially fire hundreds of bullets in a short period of time. Instantiating each of these bullets on demand is extremely inefficient, and is bound to cause massive frame rate issues. So, I designed a system that generates a sufficient amount of every object needed to be spawned before a level begins. When a bullet is fired, it is "spawned" by taking a bullet from the pool of cloned objects, revealing it, moving it to its origin position, and given a velocity and direction to move along. When the bullet ends its life cycle, it is hidden and stowed away until it is needed once more. variety.

I eventually expanded this system to be used for spawning enemies and drops to be picked up by the player, too. With this system, the limit on how many bullets the player and any enemy can fire is many magnitudes higher, which allows for more
